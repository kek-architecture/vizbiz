About

The Hungarian Contemporary Architecture Centre - KÉK and Brick Visual present The VizBiz conference dedicated to the business benefits of architecture visualization.

In order to stay competitive, architecture practices need to adapt quickly to the constantly changing professional environment. Architecture visualization (ArchViz) has the potential to positively influence and even catalyze this adaptation process. By using and developing the latest technologies such as augmented and virtual reality, architects have the freedom to showcase their work in a highly engaging way, like never before. Visual content is essential to translate ideas and convince clients, competition juries and developers.

The Hungarian Contemporary Architecture Centre - KÉK teamed up with Brick Visual, a high-end architectural visualization company to organise a full day conference dedicated to “Viz Business”. Brick Visual’s industry know-how and international connections have inspired a stunning program with great speakers at the forefront of their industry. They will tell directly about their experiences, focusing on what works and what does not, sharing what tools and processes have had a beneficial impact on their practices. The conference provides first-hand insights into how to maximise production effectiveness, optimize workflow to help you to learn how to engage with ArchViz.

On the second day of the event, the practice based VizBiz workshop at Brick Visual's office will introduce you to the latest tech developments in ArchViz and give hands-on practices to create engaging visuals with gesture-like drafts and sketches.

The accompanying exhibition, The Art of Viz Business is showcasing world-class ArchViz studios based in Budapest delivering award winning designs to top architecture firms globally.

Get to know how it can help your business at the The VizBiz Conference.

Speakers

Thorvald Aschim
Architect, Dark Arkitekter

Thorvald Aschim is PR-advisor for Dark Arkitekter with a background in journalism and communications. He has been a radio-presenter and advisor to Scandinavian RockN’Roll-investor Petter Stordalen. Joined Dark last year to help communicate the company’s vision and projects.

Erik Ege
Architect, Dark Arkitekter

Erik Ege is an architect and partner at Dark. He has been at the company since he graduated from NTNU five years ago. Erik is the architect behind the new Voss Station Hotel and has become quite the expert on digitalization within architecture. Most days Erik can be seen in a black turtleneck.

Andras Kaldos
CEO | Brick Visual

Bio

Taran Kundi
Architect, Woods Bagot

Tarran is a creative visualization artist who holds a dual Master’s degree in architecture and environmental design from the University of Nottingham.
He manages the global visual communications team and is responsible for all of Woods Bagot’s 3D visualization initiatives. His approach is based on the creation of immersive, emotive imagery at all stages of the design process, including feasibility, concept design, and marketing work. His day-to-day role includes close collaboration with both architecture and interior design teams, training, and developing integrated project workflows

Knut Ramstad
Architect MNAL | Nordic

Knut Ramstad (M. Arch) is based in Oslo, Norway. He shares his time between serving as Chief Technology Officer of Nordic — Office of Architecture, one of Scandinavia’s largest architectural firms; and professor at NTNU - Norwegian University of Science and Technology.
Since the early 90’s, Knut has been working with the development and implementation of 3D technology in larger urban developments and architectural projects; continuously striving to develop novel and effective new workflows and procedures that widens the scope for the application of 3D visualization and VR in design, decision making, quality control, and communication. Of the recent assignments are the New Government Quarter in Oslo, Stockholm Airport Arlanda, Oslo Airport Extension, Bergen Airport and New Istanbul Airport.

Aasmund Vinje
Architect, Saaha

Aasmund started his full-time employment at Saaha Arkitekter the fall of 2016, having finished his masters degree at the Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts School of Architecture, in Copenhagen, at the department of Transformation and Rehabilitation. Between 2014-2015 he worked at Saaha Arkitekter as an intern, between his 4th and 5th year in Copenhagen. Aasmund has, during his time at the office, been an important contributor in a varied range of projects and competitions
